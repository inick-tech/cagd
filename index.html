01 - Introduction

Product Shape as a Marketing Variable

Physical product geometry is not merely an engineering decision - it is a primary driver of consumer perception, purchase intent, and brand equity. The contour of a Coca-Cola bottle, the chamfered edges of an iPhone, and the hood curvature of a BMW are all strategic assets designed to trigger specific emotional responses. Computer-Aided Geometric Design (CAGD) provides the mathematical language to represent these shapes precisely through B-spline and NURBS surfaces.

The central question this research addresses: can customer data replace designer intuition as the input to CAGD?

This work represents a recent shift in CAGD toward data-driven and AI-augmented geometric modelling.

$$\mathbf{S}(u,v) = \sum_{i=0}^{n}\sum_{j=0}^{m} N_{i,p}(u)\;N_{j,q}(v)\;\mathbf{P}_{ij}, \qquad (u,v)\in[0,1]^2$$
Eq. 1 - Tensor-product B-spline surface. $N_{i,p}$, $N_{j,q}$ are Cox–de Boor basis functions of degree $p$, $q$; control point matrix $\mathbf{P}\in\mathbb{R}^{(n+1)\times(m+1)\times 3}$ fully determines the shape.

02 - Research Problem

The Semantic–Geometric Gap

Consumer preferences exist in language space - reviews, Reddit threads, social media posts - while product designs live in geometric space, parameterised by control points and knot vectors. No principled, end-to-end mathematical framework currently bridges these two domains at scale.

$$\underbrace{\mathbf{x} \in \mathcal{L}}_{\text{language}} \;\xrightarrow{\;f_\theta\;}\; \underbrace{\boldsymbol{\phi} \in \mathbb{R}^k}_{\text{preference vector}} \;\xrightarrow{\;g_\psi\;}\; \underbrace{\mathbf{P} \in \mathbb{R}^{(n+1)(m+1)\times 3}}_{\text{control point matrix}} \;\xrightarrow{\;\mathbf{S}(u,v)\;}\; \underbrace{\text{3D surface}}_{\text{geometry}}$$
Eq. 2 - Full semantic-to-geometry pipeline. $f_\theta$ = NLP encoder (BERT / RoBERTa); $g_\psi$ = geometric decoder mapping preference latent vector to control points; $\mathbf{S}$ = B-spline surface constructor.

$$\boldsymbol{\phi} = \frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{N}\operatorname{BERT}(\mathbf{x}_i)\;\in\mathbb{R}^{768}, \qquad \hat{s}_i = \sigma\!\left(\mathbf{w}^\top\boldsymbol{\phi} + b\right)\in[0,1]$$
Eq. 3 - Preference vector as mean-pooled BERT embeddings over $N$ reviews; $\hat{s}_i$ is the predicted score for design dimension $i$ (sleekness, roundness, compactness, etc.).
$$\mathcal{L}(\psi) = \underbrace{\bigl\|\boldsymbol{\phi}_{\text{target}} - \boldsymbol{\phi}(g_\psi(\boldsymbol{\phi}))\bigr\|^2}_{\text{preference alignment}} + \lambda_1\underbrace{\int_0^1\!\!\int_0^1\bigl\|\mathbf{S}_{uu}\bigr\|^2+\bigl\|\mathbf{S}_{vv}\bigr\|^2\,du\,dv}_{\text{surface fairness (thin-plate energy)}} + \lambda_2\underbrace{\sum_{i,j}\bigl\|\Delta^2\mathbf{P}_{ij}\bigr\|^2}_{\text{control net regularity}}$$
Eq. 4 - Joint loss function. Surface fairness (standard in CAGD) prevents geometrically degenerate outputs caused by noisy language signals; hyperparameters $\lambda_1,\lambda_2\ge 0$ balance aesthetic fit against geometric quality.

The geometric decoder operates directly in the NURBS/B-spline control point space, ensuring compatibility with industrial CAD pipelines.

05 - Conclusion

Geometry as a Marketing Instrument

Data-Driven Geometric Design closes the loop between consumer voice and product form. By embedding NLP pipelines into CAGD workflows, companies can treat B-spline control points as continuously optimisable marketing parameters - making product shape a data-responsive, real-time asset. This interdisciplinary framework, spanning mathematics, machine learning, and marketing analytics, defines a fertile PhD research frontier with direct implications for consumer experience optimisation and intelligent product strategy.

Future CAGD systems may evolve into fully closed-loop design platforms driven by real-time market data.
